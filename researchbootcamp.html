Programs Overview

Examples of some of the past projects done by our research bootcamp students. Explore more here.

  • Our dedication is to make research opportunities available to high school students, breaking down barriers to knowledge and experience. We have had the privilege of mentoring over 160 students from 20+ countries in 30+ unique projects in 2023. Our students have further broken barries to submit their research in Stanford’s Intersect journal, IEEE, IJSER and more. Some of our student’s work can is shown on the left.

  • Immerse yourself in the process of real-world social impact based research by delving into literature review, formulating hypotheses, designing experiments, communicating your results in a professional setting, and explore publication outreach. Engage with the rapidly growing field of STEM, Computer Science or Non-STEM research by learning the basics of academic research that has the potential to make a real world impact.

    Group Research: Research in a team of 3-6 students. Projects will be assigned by the Incognito Blueprints team and cannot be changed.

    Individual Research: Work on an individual research project under the supervision of a mentor/TA. Student will receive 3 options to select the research project.

  • The program has 1-2 weekly meetings, with optional TA run office hours available. We expect students to be able to dedicate 5-10 hours per week in total, with flexibility for further exploration. Saturday Advisor Lectures (10:30-11:30 am ET) to learn team building, communication, research fundamentals and review relevant literature for research inspiration. All students receive support from mentors/TAs throughout the week offline through slack channels if your team runs into roadblocks or want to discuss ideas.

    Group research: Students will meet with mentors/TAs as a group. No individual sessions will be permitted. Weekly sprint meetings to be conducted every Saturday 11:30 am-12:30 pm ET to discuss research updates, divide & delegate tasks using SCRUM methodology & work on assignments.

    Individual Research: Student will meet with the assigned mentor/TA individually for office hours defined within each assignment.

  • Hands-on Contribution (virtual): Guided by a dedicated mentor/TA who will facilitate progress and encourage individual engagement to enhance learning.

    Eligibility: Both bootcamps are ideal for students in class 9 or above. Open to all students from any country.

    Prerequisites: The programs are tailored to allow students to engage in real world research without prior research experience. While each team/individual will work on a unique research project, the goal of the bootcamp is to help students start, sustain & understand the research process.

    Curriculum: In addition to weekly group live sessions by the advisor, we provide pre-structured videos to help build student fundamentals. This minimizes technical hurdles, and promotes an environment of self learning with readily available resources to ensure a solid foundation for all students.

  • After purchasing either bootcamps, students will get immediate access to their student portals which can be accessed by signing in here. On the student dashboard, students will find an on-boarding form which asks details (eg. student background, profile, research category preference) required for us to either assign teams and/or research projects. Next steps regarding team allocation, live session, etc. are present under the announcements tab of the student dashboard.

🔭 Research Areas

  • STEM

    We are directing out STEM-based projects towards an emphasize on biomedical sciences for students to contribute towards real life issues in global public health making a positive impact in the society. This focus provides a unique opportunity for students with diverse interests including but not limited to physics, biology, electronics, material science and more. This research category will allow students to harness their passion and skills within a collaborative working environment with peers.

  • Computer Science

    Students will learn how to collect, clean, and manipulate data, as well as how to visualize and communicate results effectively. Most of our topics revolve around the concept of bridging the gap between application based AI, machine learning, Python, UX/UI and more to create meaning societal impact. While technical coding skills are certainly valued, our foremost goal is to transition self learned theoretical knowledge to practical real-world applications.

  • Non-STEM

    Non-STEM professionals encounter a multitude of challenges within their work, including mastering business strategies, implementing effective marketing techniques, conducting thorough SWOT analysis, understanding economic principles, all while upholding ethical standards. This bootcamp delves into such areas of expertise, offering students a pathway to enhance their understanding and skills by immersing them in real-world issues and practical projects, altogether fostering a dynamic learning environment.

Why Research?

Research is an incredibly valuable experience as it provides opportunities for personal and academic growth. It teaches critical thinking, problem solving, and effective communication skills, which are essential for success in any career or academic pursuit

Research can also be a way for students to explore potential career paths and gain exposure to the many diverse fields available to them. Moreover, it can provide them with opportunities to collaborate with professionals in the field, further their learning and develop a network of contacts for the future.

For college applications, participating in research can demonstrate a student's initiative, dedication, and ability to work independently or as part of a team. This type of extracurricular activity showcases their passion for a particular subject, and it can provide a unique and meaningful addition to their college application.

FAQs

Will I always have access to the course?

You will have unlimited access to the coursework from 06 Jan 2024 to 17 Feb 2024.

I am not applying for this admission to US college yet, is this course for me?

Absolutely. This course is designed for students who are interested in learning about how to carry out effective research in a methodological manner. The learnings from this bootcamp can be applied to any research in the future. Additionally, this research activity being unique to you, makes for a great extracurricular activity that you can add to your college applications whenever the time comes.

How do the live sessions work?

All enrolled students will be notified of the live sessions through their student dashboards and slack groups.

Does this course include one-on-one Live sessions with a mentor?

Unfortunately no. The Live sessions are grouped, however if you are looking for one-on-one assistance, feel free to check out any one of our mentorship programs as ALL of them are one-on-one.

Can I get a refund if I am not satisfied with the bootcamp?

Unfortunately, at this point because of how the bootcamp is structured for a small group, we cannot offer refunds.

Since the price is in Indian rupees, do I need to have an Indian card to purchase?

You can pay using any credit card across our website. Your bank will automatically convert to the necessary currency.

I want to use another payment option or my payment option is not working, who do I contact?

Please reach out to us at info@incognitoblueprints.com for exploring payment options like UPI, GooglePay, Paytm, etc.
